Many Spanish verbs follow a fixed pattern for each tense. These patterns combine either the verb's "infinitive" form or its "stem" with a specific ending for each "person."

Verbs that follow these patterns are known as strong"regular verbs," and simply learning the pattern for each tense will give you access to thousands of verbs!

A verb's infinitive is its basic form — meaning the verb isn't modified to match, say, a tense or person. We often precede a verb's infinitive with "to." The infitive (or "to") forms of the verbs we'll use in this article are:

  • "hablar," meaning "to talk/speak,"
  • "comer," meaning "to eat,"
  • and "vivir," meaning "to live."
